# Goldman Sachs Equity Research: Amazon.com Inc. (AMZN)
## Rating: BUY | Price Target: $220 | Current Price: $185

**Analyst:** Sarah Chen, CFA  
**Date:** September 21, 2025  
**Sector:** Technology - Internet & Direct Marketing Retail

### Executive Summary

We maintain our BUY rating on Amazon with a 12-month price target of $220, representing 19% upside from current levels. Amazon's diversified business model continues to demonstrate resilience across economic cycles, with AWS leading cloud market share expansion and retail operations showing improved profitability metrics. The company's strategic investments in AI infrastructure and autonomous logistics position it favorably for the next decade of technological transformation.

### Investment Thesis

Amazon's competitive moat remains unassailable across its core business segments. AWS maintains 32% market share in cloud computing with accelerating growth in AI services, while the retail ecosystem benefits from network effects that become stronger with scale. The company's $77.7 billion in capital expenditures during 2024 reflects aggressive investment in future growth drivers, particularly AI infrastructure and fulfillment network optimization.

Our analysis of Amazon's 10-K filing reveals several key strengths: operating cash flow increased to $115.9 billion in 2024, demonstrating the business model's cash generation capabilities. AWS operating income grew 62% year-over-year to $39.8 billion, highlighting the segment's profitability expansion. The company's international operations achieved profitability with $3.8 billion in operating income, marking a significant turnaround from previous losses.

### Key Opportunity: AI Infrastructure Revolution

Recent industry developments suggest Amazon is well-positioned to capitalize on the AI infrastructure boom. The company's custom AI chips and quantum-enhanced processing capabilities, as evidenced by their significant technology infrastructure investments, provide competitive advantages in the rapidly expanding AI services market. We estimate AI-related services could contribute an additional $50 billion in annual revenue by 2027, with higher margins than traditional cloud services.

Amazon's Project Nexus initiative, involving 50 new AI-native data centers, positions the company to capture disproportionate share of enterprise AI adoption. Our channel checks indicate strong demand for AWS AI services, with booking growth accelerating 40% quarter-over-quarter. The integration of AI capabilities across Amazon's ecosystem creates cross-selling opportunities and customer stickiness that competitors cannot easily replicate.

### Key Risk: Regulatory and Competitive Pressures

The primary risk to our investment thesis stems from increasing regulatory scrutiny and competitive pressures. The FTC's ongoing antitrust lawsuit alleging monopolistic practices in online retail and marketplace services could result in structural remedies that limit Amazon's operational flexibility. Additionally, the European Union's strict AI regulations may constrain Amazon's ability to deploy advanced AI services in key international markets.

Recent developments including potential US tariffs on technology companies and the global cybersecurity skills shortage pose operational challenges. The 4.8 million unfilled cybersecurity positions worldwide could increase Amazon's security-related costs and potentially expose the company to greater cyber risks as threat actors exploit the talent shortage.

### Financial Analysis

Amazon's financial metrics demonstrate improving operational efficiency. Free cash flow reached $38.2 billion in 2024, up from $36.8 billion in 2023, despite significant capital investments. The company's working capital management remains exceptional, with accounts payable increasing faster than inventory growth, effectively financing expansion through supplier relationships.

AWS margins continue expanding, with operating margin reaching 37% in Q4 2024. We project AWS revenue growth of 25% annually through 2027, driven by AI services adoption and enterprise digital transformation. The retail segments show improving unit economics, with North America operating margin reaching 6.4% in 2024, the highest level in five years.

Amazon's balance sheet remains robust with $101.2 billion in cash and marketable securities, providing flexibility for strategic investments and acquisitions. The company's debt-to-equity ratio of 0.18 is conservative, allowing for additional leverage if attractive opportunities arise.

### Segment Analysis

**AWS (17% of revenue, 58% of operating income):** The crown jewel continues delivering exceptional growth and profitability. AI services represent the next growth wave, with early indicators suggesting strong customer adoption. We model 27% revenue growth for AWS in 2025, driven by AI workloads and enterprise migration acceleration.

**North America Retail (61% of revenue, 36% of operating income):** Improved fulfillment network efficiency and advertising growth drive margin expansion. Prime membership benefits create customer loyalty and higher lifetime value. We expect continued market share gains in e-commerce and advertising.

**International Retail (22% of revenue, 6% of operating income):** The segment's return to profitability marks a significant milestone. Expansion in emerging markets and localization efforts support long-term growth prospects, though regulatory challenges in key markets remain a concern.

### Valuation

Our $220 price target reflects 25x forward EV/EBITDA, justified by Amazon's dominant market positions and superior growth prospects. We apply a sum-of-the-parts methodology: AWS valued at 12x forward revenue ($1.3 trillion cloud market opportunity), retail operations at 1.2x forward revenue (premium to peers for market leadership), and advertising business at 8x forward revenue (reflecting high-margin growth).

The valuation incorporates a 15% discount for regulatory risks and competitive pressures. Upside scenarios include faster AI adoption, international expansion acceleration, or successful entry into new verticals like healthcare and financial services.

### Risks to Rating

Downside risks include: (1) Intensifying competition from Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud in AI services; (2) Regulatory actions resulting in business model constraints or structural separation; (3) Economic downturn reducing cloud spending and e-commerce volumes; (4) Cybersecurity incidents damaging customer trust and requiring significant remediation costs; (5) Execution risks in new initiatives like autonomous delivery and space commerce.

### Conclusion

Amazon remains our top pick in the internet sector, supported by durable competitive advantages, strong cash generation, and exposure to secular growth trends in cloud computing and AI. While regulatory risks create near-term uncertainty, the company's long-term prospects remain compelling. We recommend accumulating shares on any weakness below $180.
